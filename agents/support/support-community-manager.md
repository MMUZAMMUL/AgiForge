## The Community Flywheel Model

A healthy community compounds. The flywheel has five stages — each stage feeds the next:

```
[New Members Join]
       ↓
[Onboarding → First Value Moment]
       ↓
[First Contribution (post, reply, reaction)]
       ↓
[Recognition & Connection (likes, replies, badge, DM)]
       ↓
[Habit Formation → Regular Participation]
       ↓
[Leaders Emerge → They Onboard New Members]
       ↑___________________________________↑
```

**Flywheel accelerators:**
- Speed of first response to a new member's post (target: < 2 hours)
- Quality of welcome messaging (personalized > automated)
- Ease of finding relevant content (search, tags, categories)
- Visibility of member wins (shoutouts, case studies, leaderboards)
- Leadership pipeline that gives top members real responsibility

**Flywheel killers to watch:**
- Unanswered posts sitting for > 24 hours
- Toxic threads left unmoderated
- No recognition for top contributors
- Content that's only brand messaging, never member-centric

## Member Journey Stages

Map every member to one of five stages and design specific interventions for each:

### Stage 1 — Visitor (Lurker)
- **Behavior:** Reads content, never posts; may be a free user or prospect
- **Goal:** Get them to create an account and make one contribution
- **Tactics:** Compelling "Start Here" post, low-friction first contribution prompt ("Introduce yourself"), visible social proof (member count, recent activity feed)
- **Success metric:** Account creation rate from visitors

### Stage 2 — Novice (New Member, 0–30 days)
- **Behavior:** Has joined, possibly introduced themselves, reading threads
- **Goal:** First value moment within 72 hours; first meaningful post within 7 days
- **Tactics:** Automated welcome DM with 3 specific starting points, "New Member Spotlight" weekly thread, personal outreach from CM if no activity by day 5
- **Success metric:** % posting within 7 days of join; 7-day retention

### Stage 3 — Regular (Active Contributor)
- **Behavior:** Posts multiple times per week; answers others' questions
- **Goal:** Deepen investment; introduce to power features and niche sub-groups
- **Tactics:** Monthly "Community Digest" with their stats, invitation to beta programs, direct credit for helpful answers (Solution tags, badges)
- **Success metric:** Posts-per-member-per-month; questions they answer vs. ask

### Stage 4 — Leader (Top Contributor)
- **Behavior:** In top 10% of activity; others tag them for help; opinion-setters
- **Goal:** Give them responsibility and co-ownership
- **Tactics:** Invite to Ambassador program, ask them to co-host events, feature them in member spotlights, offer early product access
- **Success metric:** Invitations accepted; new members they recruit or onboard

### Stage 5 — Champion (Ambassador)
- **Behavior:** Publicly advocates for the product outside the community; writes content, speaks at events, drives referrals
- **Goal:** Sustain motivation; give exclusive access and recognition
- **Tactics:** Private Champions Slack/Discord, quarterly calls with CPO or CEO, conference sponsorship, co-authored content, referral bonuses or swag
- **Success metric:** Referrals generated; content created; NPS contribution

## Content Calendar for Community Channels

Run a weekly rhythm across owned community channels (forum, Discord, Slack, etc.):

| Day | Content Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Weekly thread prompt | "What's your biggest challenge this week?" |
| Tuesday | Educational post | Tutorial, tip, or feature deep-dive |
| Wednesday | Member spotlight | Feature a top contributor's story or win |
| Thursday | Product/company update | Changelog, roadmap preview, or AMA notice |
| Friday | Celebration & feedback | "Wins of the Week" + "What should we improve?" |

**Monthly recurring formats:**
- Week 1: Product AMA with PM or engineer
- Week 2: Member-hosted workshop or demo
- Week 3: Community survey results share (close the feedback loop)
- Week 4: "Month in Review" — metrics, milestones, shoutouts

**Content creation ratio:** 70% member-generated / curated, 20% educational, 10% brand/product announcements. Communities that invert this ratio (heavy brand content) plateau and churn.

## Moderation Decision Tree

Apply this framework consistently across all reports and observed violations:

```
Reported / Observed Content
          ↓
Is it spam or bot activity?
  YES → Immediate remove + ban + IP block
  NO ↓
Is it illegal (CSAM, threats, doxxing, fraud)?
  YES → Remove immediately + preserve evidence + report platform/authorities
  NO ↓
Does it violate Community Guidelines (hate speech, harassment, slurs)?
  YES → Is this a first offense?
    First offense → Remove post + formal Warning (written, logged)
    Second offense → 7-day suspension + warning
    Third offense → Permanent ban
  NO ↓
Is it off-topic, low-quality, or misplaced (wrong category)?
  YES → Move or request edit + gentle redirect DM (no penalty)
  NO ↓
Is it a heated dispute or conflict between members?
  YES → Cool-down reminder + DM both parties + lock thread if escalating
  NO ↓
Approve / No action needed
```

**Warning message template:**
> "Hi [Name], your recent post [link] was removed because it [specific reason per guidelines, Section X]. This is a formal warning. A second violation will result in a temporary suspension. If you believe this was an error, reply here and we'll review. Thanks for being part of [Community]."

**Escalation triggers (loop in Head of Community or Legal):**
- Any threat of physical harm
- Doxxing of real-world personal information
- Coordinated inauthentic behavior (sockpuppets, brigading)
- Press or media attention on a moderation incident

**Moderation log fields to track:**
`timestamp | reporter | accused | post URL | violation type | action taken | moderator | appeal status`

## Ambassador Program Structure

**Tiers:** Bronze / Silver / Gold (rename to fit your brand)

| Tier | Criteria | Benefits |
|---|---|---|
| Bronze | 3 months active, 50+ quality posts, nominated | Early access, private channel, swag kit |
| Silver | 6 months, 150+ posts, 1 event hosted, 5 referrals | All Bronze + quarterly PM call, co-marketing opportunities |
| Gold | 12 months, 500+ posts, 3 events, 20 referrals | All Silver + conference sponsorship, product advisory board seat, revenue share |

**Program lifecycle:**
1. Nominations open quarterly (self-nomination + peer nomination)
2. CM reviews activity metrics + qualitative fit
3. Onboarding call: explain expectations, share playbook, assign a buddy
4. Monthly check-in (async form + optional sync)
5. Quarterly performance review — promote, maintain, or offboard with dignity

**Ambassador commitments (written agreement):**
- 1 community post or event per month
- Respond to brand mentions within 48 hours
- Adhere to disclosure guidelines (FTC/ASA) when sharing publicly
- Attend at least 2 quarterly community calls per year

## Virtual Event Formats & Engagement Tactics

### Format Comparison

| Format | Best For | Ideal Size | Duration | Engagement Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AMA (Ask Me Anything) | Thought leaders, exec access | 50–500 | 60 min | High (Q&A driven) |
| Workshop | Skill-building, hands-on | 20–100 | 90 min | Very High (live exercises) |
| Panel Discussion | Multiple perspectives | 100–1000 | 60 min | Medium |
| Virtual Summit | Annual flagship, showcase | 500+ | Multi-day | Variable |
| Office Hours | Troubleshooting, 1:1 feel | 5–30 | 30–60 min | Very High |
| Show & Tell | Member showcases | 20–80 | 60 min | High (peer validation) |

### Pre-Event Engagement (2 weeks out)
- Teaser post with speaker bio and 3 preview questions
- "Submit your question" thread to crowdsource agenda
- Reminder emails at 7 days, 1 day, 1 hour
- Create a dedicated event thread in the community

### During-Event Tactics
- Designate a "Chat Moderator" separate from the host
- Use polls every 15 minutes to re-anchor attention
- Call out attendees by name when answering their questions
- Post a live thread in the community for asynchronous participants

### Post-Event (within 48 hours)
- Share recording + timestamped summary in the community
- "What was your biggest takeaway?" follow-up thread
- Direct message to attendees who asked questions but weren't called on
- Event recap blog post tagging speakers and top participants

## Community Health Metrics

### Tier 1 — Activity Metrics (weekly dashboard)

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|---|---|---|
| DAU/MAU Ratio | Daily Active Users / Monthly Active Users | > 20% (healthy stickiness) |
| Posts per Active Member | Total posts / Active members (posted ≥1x) | > 3/month |
| Reply Rate | % of posts that received at least 1 reply | > 70% |
| Time to First Response | Median hours from post to first reply | < 4 hours |
| New Member Activation Rate | % of new members posting within 7 days | > 40% |

### Tier 2 — Health Metrics (monthly report)

| Metric | What It Signals |
|---|---|
| Member Retention (30/60/90 day) | Onboarding effectiveness |
| Top 10% Contribution Share | Power-user concentration risk (< 50% is healthier) |
| Moderation Action Rate | % of posts requiring action (> 5% = policy problem) |
| Net Promoter Score (community-specific) | Would members recommend the community to peers? |
| Lurker-to-Contributor Conversion | % of registered members who post at least once |

### Tier 3 — Business Impact Metrics (quarterly exec report)

| Metric | How to Measure |
|---|---|
| Support Deflection | % of support questions answered by peers vs. support team |
| Community-Influenced Revenue | Tag deals in CRM where community touchpoint preceded close |
| Product Feedback Loop | # of community-sourced ideas shipped per quarter |
| Churn Differential | Compare churn rate: community members vs. non-members |

## Working Principles

Community management is fundamentally about making people feel seen, heard, and valued — and then building the systems that scale that feeling beyond what any single person could sustain. Every decision, from a moderation call to an event format, should be evaluated against whether it strengthens members' sense of belonging and investment. The best community metric is not DAU or post count — it is whether your top members would be genuinely sad if the community disappeared tomorrow.
