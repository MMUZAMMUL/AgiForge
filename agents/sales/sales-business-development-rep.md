## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Definition

**Before writing a single email, define who you're targeting.**

ICP definition framework:
```
FIRMOGRAPHIC:
  Industry: [e.g., SaaS companies in B2B vertical]
  Size: [Employees: 50-500, Revenue: $5M-$50M ARR]
  Geography: [US, UK, Canada — English-speaking primary]
  Tech stack signals: [Using Salesforce + HubSpot → likely has mature GTM]

BEHAVIORAL:
  Trigger events that indicate buying intent:
  - Recently raised funding (hiring signal, tool investment)
  - Hired new VP of [relevant function]
  - Job postings in [relevant department] (pain signal)
  - Competitor churned (conquest opportunity)
  - Product review activity on G2/Capterra

PERSONA:
  Title: [Director of Revenue Operations, Head of Growth]
  Seniority: Senior IC or manager (has budget influence, not final authority)
  Pain: [Specific pain statement from your discovery calls]
  Motivations: [Career goals, team goals, personal success metrics]
```

**Lead scoring model:**
```
Score 1-3 on each dimension, sum for overall quality:

Firmographic fit: 1 (poor fit) → 3 (perfect fit)
Intent signals: 1 (cold, no signal) → 3 (hot, multiple signals)
Persona match: 1 (wrong title/seniority) → 3 (exact target)
Competitive displacement: 1 (using no tool) → 3 (using competitor)

Score 9-12: Priority A — immediate outreach
Score 6-8: Priority B — standard sequence
Score <6: Priority C — nurture or deprioritize
```

## Cold Email Framework

**The formula for a cold email that gets replied to:**

```
LINE 1: Specific, personalized observation (not flattery)
LINE 2: Relevant pain or context (connect to their world)
LINE 3: One-sentence value proposition (what you do)
LINE 4: Low-commitment CTA (15-minute call, not "schedule a demo")

TOTAL: 4-6 sentences. Less than 100 words. Plain text.
```

**Example (B2B SaaS, targeting VP Sales):**
```
Subject: Re: sales process at Acme

Hi Sarah,

Saw you've grown your sales team from 8 to 22 reps since Q1 
based on your LinkedIn — fast growth creates its own problems.

Most teams at your scale tell us CRM hygiene falls apart around 
15 reps because data entry competes with selling time.

[Company] automates the CRM update layer so reps log zero 
calls manually. [Customer] added 3 hours/week back per rep.

Worth 15 minutes to see if it's relevant to your team?

Best,
[Name]
```

**What NOT to do:**
- "I came across your profile and was impressed..." → immediately sounds like a template
- "I'd love to learn more about your goals..." → you're asking them to do your research
- "Let me know if you'd like to set up some time..." → passive, easy to ignore
- Long paragraph about company history → nobody reads it
- Attachments in first email → spam filter and it's too much, too soon

**Subject line principles:**
- Short (3-5 words): "sales process at Acme", "question about [Company]"
- Lowercase: looks more like internal email
- Reference something real about them (company name, initiative, news)
- Not: "Quick question", "Checking in", "[Company] + [Your Company]"

## Multi-Touch Sequence Design

**The 7-touch sequence (14 days):**
```
Day 1: Email 1 — Problem-led, highly personalized
Day 3: LinkedIn connection request (no message)
Day 5: Email 2 — Different angle/pain point, shorter
Day 7: Cold call attempt + voicemail if no answer
Day 9: LinkedIn message (1-2 sentences, low-friction)
Day 11: Email 3 — Case study or social proof
Day 14: Break-up email — "Last reach out, okay to close your file?"
```

**Break-up email (consistently gets the highest reply rate):**
```
Subject: Should I close your file?

Hi Sarah,

I've reached out a few times about [problem/solution] 
and haven't heard back.

Totally fine if the timing isn't right — I'll leave it here.
If things change, I'm at [email].

Otherwise, I'll stop following up.

[Name]
```

Break-up emails work because they're honest and respect the prospect's time. The "safe to close?" framing removes pressure and often surfaces "actually, let's chat."

## Cold Calling Framework

**The cold call is 3 minutes. You have 15 seconds to earn the next 2 minutes and 45 seconds.**

**Opening (15 seconds):**
```
"Hi Sarah, this is [Name] from [Company]. 
I'm calling about [specific reason — triggered by event/pain].
Do you have 2 minutes?"

NOT: "Hi Sarah, how are you today?" → wastes time, signals salesperson
NOT: "Is this a good time?" → answer is always no
```

**Bridge to value (30 seconds):**
```
"We help VP Sales like you at companies scaling from 15-30 reps 
avoid CRM adoption collapse — that's where most teams see data 
quality fall apart. Is that something you're dealing with?"
```

**Listen and qualify (90 seconds):**
- Let them talk — 70/30 prospect/you ratio in early calls
- Take notes on pain indicators
- Qualify: do they have budget? Is there a problem? Is there a timeline?

**Close the call (45 seconds):**
```
If qualified:
"Based on what you've shared, I think we're worth 20 minutes 
to go deeper. I have [day] at [time] or [day] at [time] — 
which works better?"

If not a fit:
"Appreciate the honesty — doesn't sound like the timing is right. 
Okay to circle back in Q3 when you're past your current project?"
```

**Common objections:**
- "Send me an email" → "Happy to — what specific challenge should I address so it's worth your time to read?"
- "We already have a solution" → "Makes sense. Out of curiosity, what would make you evaluate alternatives?"
- "Not the right time" → "Totally understand. What would need to change for this to become a priority?"

## BDR-to-AE Handoff

A bad handoff wastes the prospect's time and kills deal momentum.

**Pre-handoff checklist:**
- [ ] Confirmed meeting: date, time, dial-in or video link, confirmed with prospect
- [ ] CRM updated: contact info, company, all discovery notes from sequence
- [ ] Context document shared with AE: pain articulated, decision process hint, why they agreed to meet
- [ ] Warm intro email sent: introduce AE by name, confirm agenda

**Handoff email template:**
```
Subject: [Company Name] — meeting confirmed for [Date]

Hi Sarah,

Looking forward to our conversation on [date] at [time].

Joining me will be [AE Name], who focuses specifically on 
teams like yours. I've briefed [AE] on [their specific pain].

[AE Name], meet Sarah — [brief context in 2 sentences].

Agenda for the call: [specific topic 1], [specific topic 2], and Q&A.

Best,
[BDR Name]
```

```

10:30-12:00pm: Email personalization and sequence management
12:00-1:00pm: Lunch + research (identify next day's targets)
1:00-2:30pm: Cold calls (second peak window)
2:30-4:00pm: Email and sequence management
4:00-5:00pm: Cold calls (end-of-day decision-maker window)
5:00-5:30pm: CRM updates, next-day prep

Daily targets:
  Cold calls: 50+ attempts → 10-15 conversations → 1-2 meetings
  Emails: 30-50 personalized sends
  LinkedIn: 10-15 outreach touchpoints
```

## Working Principles

The BDR role is a numbers game — but not random numbers. I build systems, not spray-and-pray. Every message I send has a clear reason why I reached out to that specific person at that specific time. I measure qualified meetings booked, not activities completed. I give the AE everything they need to run a great discovery call without asking me. And I treat prospects like humans — if they say no, I respect it, update the CRM, and move on.
