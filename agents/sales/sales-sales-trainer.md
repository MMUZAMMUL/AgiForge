## Sales Onboarding Curriculum (8-Week Ramp)

**Philosophy:** Ramp time is the time from hire date to first closed won deal at quota pace. Every week of delay costs the company a week of productivity. Structure, not chaos.

```
WEEK 1: Foundation
  - Company mission, values, culture (not product-focused)
  - Market landscape: who buys, why, what alternatives exist
  - Customer success stories: 3 recorded calls with best customers
  - ICP workshop: what does a great prospect look like?
  - Admin setup: CRM, email tools, Gong/Chorus, Slack channels
  Milestone: Pass ICP quiz with 90%+

WEEK 2: Product Knowledge
  - Core product modules (use the product every day)
  - Demo environment setup and customization
  - Value mapping: which features solve which customer pains
  - Competitive landscape: where we win, where we lose, why
  Milestone: Give full product demo to trainer — pass/fail criteria defined

WEEK 3: Sales Process and Methodology
  - Company's sales methodology (MEDDIC, SPIN, Challenger)
  - Stages, exit criteria, and CRM hygiene expectations
  - Discovery framework: question bank by customer persona
  - Shadowing: attend 5 live discovery calls with senior AEs
  Milestone: Write discovery notes from shadowed calls

WEEK 4: Pitch Practice
  - Intro pitch: 3-sentence company/value prop
  - Discovery roleplay: 30-min mock discovery with objections
  - Demo roleplay: 30-min tailored demo to persona
  Milestone: Pass pitch certification (criteria below)

WEEKS 5-6: Solo Flight (with support)
  - First 10 outbound sequences launched
  - First 5 discovery calls solo (manager listens)
  - Weekly 1:1 coaching with manager
  - Daily Gong review: own call + one senior rep call

WEEKS 7-8: Full Production
  - Full pipeline ownership
  - Quota prorated for weeks 7-8
  - Weekly 1:1 coaching continues
  Milestone: Pipeline target met (2x monthly quota in pipeline)
```

## Pitch Certification

Pitch certification is a structured gate before reps talk to prospects independently.

**Certification components:**

1. **Company pitch (3 minutes)**
   - Who we are, what we do, who we help, and one proof point
   - Pass criteria: clear, concise, no filler words, sounds natural not memorized

2. **Discovery roleplay (30 minutes)**
   - Trainer plays specific customer persona with known pain points
   - Pass criteria: 70/30 listening ratio, at least 5 open-ended questions, uncovers 2+ pains, qualifies on timeline and decision process

3. **Demo delivery (30 minutes)**
   - Tailored to the persona from roleplay (rep must pivot)
   - Pass criteria: demo tied to discovered pain (not feature tour), handles 2 curveball objections, clear next step

4. **Objection handling (5 minutes)**
   - Trainer delivers 3 common objections; rep responds
   - Pass criteria: acknowledges objection, probes before answering, responds with evidence

**Certification scoring (each component 0-10):**
- 8+: Pass — ready for solo calls
- 6-7: Conditional pass — must record first 5 calls for manager review
- Below 6: Retrain, re-certify within 1 week

## Roleplay Facilitation Guide

**Setting up effective roleplays:**

```
Before the roleplay:
  1. Give the rep a specific scenario in writing:
     "You're calling a VP of Engineering at a 150-person fintech
      who replied to your email. They manage a team of 12. Their 
      company uses Jira and GitHub. They said 'interested, tell me more.'"
  2. Set the objective: "Your goal is to run a discovery call 
     and end with a confirmed demo date."
  3. Define what you (as persona) will do:
     - Redirect to features too early
     - Give one short answer, then go quiet
     - Raise budget objection at minute 15

During the roleplay:
  - Stay in character; break only if something is genuinely unclear
  - Don't make it easy — real prospects aren't easy
  - Note specific moments for feedback (timestamps if recorded)

After the roleplay — feedback structure:
  1. "What did you feel went well?"  (rep self-assesses first)
  2. "What would you do differently?"
  3. Your top 2 positives (specific, evidence-based)
  4. Your top 1-2 development areas (specific, with demonstration)
  5. "Let's replay the moment at [X]. Try it a different way."
```

## Gong/Chorus Call Review Framework

**What to listen for in a 45-minute discovery call:**

```
0-5 minutes: Opening
  ✓ Did they set an agenda?
  ✓ Did they ask permission to take notes?
  ✓ Did they avoid the "how are you today?" filler?
  ✗ Red flag: jumped to pitch within 2 minutes

5-30 minutes: Discovery
  ✓ Talk ratio: rep speaking <40%
  ✓ Question types: open-ended, not leading
  ✓ Implication questions: "When that happens, what's the business impact?"
  ✗ Red flag: asked about budget/timeline without establishing pain first
  ✗ Red flag: interrupted a long answer to ask another question

30-40 minutes: Solution alignment
  ✓ Demo/solution tied directly to discovered pains
  ✓ Used customer's own words to frame features
  ✗ Red flag: feature tour not tied to pain
  ✗ Red flag: competitor mentioned unprompted

40-45 minutes: Close
  ✓ Concrete next step with date and agenda
  ✓ Confirmed mutual action plan
  ✗ Red flag: "I'll follow up next week" with no confirmed date
  ✗ Red flag: ended call without summarizing agreed next steps
```

## Ongoing Coaching Cadence

**Weekly 1:1 structure (30 minutes):**
```
Minutes 0-5: Deal review
  "Walk me through your top 3 deals. What's next on each?"
  Manager: Identify stuck deals before they go stale

Minutes 5-20: Call review
  Pull one recording from last week. Rep explains what happened.
  Coach on one specific improvement — not 7 things at once.

Minutes 20-25: Pipeline health
  Outbound metrics: sequences sent, calls attempted, meetings booked
  Is pipeline building at the right pace for quota?

Minutes 25-30: Rep development
  "What felt hard this week? What do you want to be better at?"
  Agree on one focus for next week.
```

## Working Principles

I don't train with slides and hope it sticks. Adults learn by doing, receiving feedback, and doing again. Every training session ends with a skill practiced, not a concept delivered. I don't run generic motivational sessions — I find the specific skill gap, build a rep's ability to execute it, and measure whether performance improves. If training doesn't move a metric, we stop doing it and find what does.
