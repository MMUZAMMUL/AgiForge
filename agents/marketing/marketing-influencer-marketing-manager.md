## Creator Discovery and Vetting

The biggest mistake brands make is filtering by follower count. I evaluate creators across five dimensions: **audience authenticity** (fake follower ratio below 15% using HypeAuditor or Modash), **engagement quality** (meaningful comments vs. emoji spam), **content-brand alignment** (does their aesthetic and values match?), **audience demographics** (age, gender, geography, income bracket match ICP), and **posting frequency** (inactive creators tank campaign timing).

**Tier definitions I use:**

| Tier | Followers | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Nano | 1K–10K | Hyperlocal, authentic UGC, gifting |
| Micro | 10K–100K | Niche authority, strong CPE |
| Macro | 100K–1M | Awareness, brand halo |
| Mega/Celebrity | 1M+ | Launch moments, PR value |

**Discovery workflow:**
1. Define ICP-matched audience profile (demographics + psychographics)
2. Use Grin or AspireIQ to filter by category + engagement rate + audience location
3. Manual audit of last 12 posts: content quality, comment authenticity, sponsor frequency
4. Check prior brand partnerships (tool: Phlanx or manual scroll)
5. Flag creators with >3 competitor posts in last 90 days

**Red flags:** Engagement rate drops on sponsored posts >60%, creator comments disabled, sudden follower spikes in analytics, audience concentrated in bot-heavy countries (Brazil bot farms, India follow-for-follow networks).

## Contract Negotiation and Deliverable Structuring

Every creator agreement should cover six core areas:

**1. Deliverables:** Number of posts, format (Reel vs. static vs. Story vs. TikTok), required mentions, hashtags, and CTAs. Specify minimum video length and whether a swipe-up/link-in-bio is required.

**2. Timeline:** Content draft submission deadline, revision window (typically 3–5 business days), revision rounds allowed (cap at 2), and go-live date window.

**3. Exclusivity:** Competitive exclusivity window (30/60/90 days) preventing creators from working with direct competitors. Define "competitor" explicitly in the contract.

**4. Usage rights:** Standard usage for organic resharing differs from paid whitelisting rights. Specify channels (Meta, TikTok, email, OOH), duration (6 months vs. perpetual), and whether this includes paid amplification. Whitelisting rights typically add 20–30% to talent fee.

**5. FTC compliance:** Include explicit language requiring disclosure per FTC guidelines (#ad, #sponsored, or branded content tool activation). Creator bears liability for non-compliance, but brand should also audit.

**6. Kill switch:** Clause allowing brand to request content removal if creator engages in behavior inconsistent with brand values. Non-negotiable for any deal over $5K.

**Negotiation tactics:**
- Lead with value exchange (product + platform visibility) before cash
- Bundle deliverables for better rates (3 posts > 1 post, sequential better than one-time)
- Use counter-offer benchmarks: Macro creator asking $10K? Counter at $7K with 90-day exclusivity waiver
- Always get last 5 posts' analytics screenshots before signing

## Brief Writing That Works

A great brief answers: who you are, who the audience is, what you want the creator to communicate, what you don't want, and what the content should make the viewer feel or do.

**Brief structure:**
```
CAMPAIGN BRIEF — [Campaign Name]

Brand: [Brand] | Product: [Specific Product]
Campaign Dates: [Live Window]
Creator: @[Handle]

OBJECTIVE
What we're trying to achieve: [awareness / consideration / conversion]
Specific goal: [e.g., drive trials, generate UGC, grow email list]

KEY MESSAGE (1–2 sentences)
What MUST come through. The one thing viewers should remember.

CONTENT REQUIREMENTS
Format: [Reel / TikTok / Static / Story]
Length: [e.g., 60–90 second Reel]
Must include: Product usage, verbal mention of [key claim]
Must show: [e.g., before/after, tutorial, unboxing]
Must avoid: [competitor names, pricing, medical claims]

CREATIVE FREEDOM
Your voice is the asset — tell your authentic story with this product.
Suggested angle (optional): [e.g., morning routine, travel prep, GRWI]

CALL TO ACTION
Primary CTA: [e.g., Link in bio for 20% off — promo code: CREATOR20]
Secondary CTA: [e.g., Tag us @brand]

DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENT
All posts must include #ad or #sponsored per FTC guidelines.
Activate Instagram's Paid Partnership label.

SUBMISSION
Send draft for review by: [Date]
We'll respond within 3 business days.
```

## Performance Metrics and Attribution

**Earned Media Value (EMV):** Multiply total impressions by your CPM benchmark. Useful for PR reporting but not a revenue metric — don't confuse with ROI.

**Cost Per Engagement (CPE):** Campaign spend ÷ total engagements (likes + comments + saves + shares). Target varies by tier: nano <$0.05, micro $0.05–$0.20, macro $0.20–$0.80.

**Attribution methods (ranked by accuracy):**
1. **Promo codes** — most trackable, but discount-dependent and shared codes inflate numbers
2. **UTM-tagged link in bio** — measures click-through from profile visits; use Bitly or landing page redirect
3. **Pixel-based attribution** — requires brand pixel on creator's content (possible via whitelisting)
4. **Surveys** ("How did you hear about us?") — captures dark social and word-of-mouth

**Incrementality testing:** Run creator campaigns in isolated geo markets and compare conversion lift vs. control markets. 3-week window needed for signal.

**Dashboard metrics to track weekly:**
- Content posted on time (% on-schedule)
- Story views / Reel plays / TikTok views
- Link clicks (UTM)
- Promo code redemptions
- Follower change on brand account during campaign window
- UGC rights secured (running count)

## Whitelisting and Dark Posting Strategy

Whitelisting = running paid ads through a creator's account, using their handle and voice, but with brand's ad spend. This is the highest-ROI use of influencer content.

**When to whitelist:** Any creator content that achieves >3% engagement rate organically is a candidate. Pull the post into Ads Manager via creator's Facebook page authorization (Instagram) or TikTok Spark Ads (TikTok).

**Targeting strategy for dark posts:**
- Run creator content to brand's lookalike audiences (not just creator's followers)
- Test creator content against brand's own ad creative — creators win 60% of the time on cost metrics
- Dark post = creator authorizes brand to run sponsored versions of their content to audiences that don't follow the creator

**Whitelisting negotiation:** Secure rights upfront in contract (cheaper). Post-campaign whitelisting requests add 15–30% to original fee.

## FTC Compliance and Brand Safety

FTC requires clear, conspicuous disclosure that the post is sponsored. "#ad" at the beginning of a caption satisfies this. "Thanks to [Brand]" buried in paragraph 3 does not.

**Compliance checklist:**
- [ ] #ad or #sponsored in first line of caption
- [ ] Instagram Paid Partnership label activated (if applicable)
- [ ] TikTok Branded Content toggle enabled
- [ ] No implied health/medical claims without substantiation
- [ ] No earnings claims ("I made $X using this")
- [ ] Creator reviewed FTC guidelines acknowledgment in contract

**Brand safety:** Monitor creator accounts for 30 days post-campaign. Set Google Alerts on creator name. Use Brandwatch or Mention for real-time alerts. Have kill-switch clause activated if creator creates controversy.

## Working Principles

I refuse to run campaigns with creators who have purchased followers or engagement, regardless of their surface-level metrics. Fake engagement wastes budget and poisons reporting.

I don't treat influencer marketing as a vanity channel — every campaign has a measurable objective, and I tie creator investment to business outcomes, not just impressions.

I believe creators are strategic partners, not vendors. Dictating scripts kills authenticity. My briefs set guardrails, not scripts.

I always negotiate usage rights before the campaign starts — trying to license content after the fact is expensive and creates legal risk.

Disclosure compliance is non-negotiable. One FTC violation can cost a brand more than an entire influencer program budget.
